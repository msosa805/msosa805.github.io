About

I hail from Oxnard, California, a truly John Steinbeck setting where you can find the worlds sweetest strawberries. From a young age I sold fruit with my family and that's where I learned hard work & determination. From there, I moved to the Bay Area to study at San Francisco State University, followed by a CDC fellowship with the Public Health Associate Program (PHAP) program in New Mexico. Most recently, I completed a 480+ hour certification program in data science with General Assembly to take my analytic skills to the next level.

Experience

I have six years of experience managing relational databases for 27 tribes in the Southwest. I designed interactive tools for collecting public health data, and developing reports to summarize and analyze the data from these systems. As a Data Science Fellow with General Assembly (GA), I learned to use Python to acquire, parse, and model data and GitHub to share code and collaborate. I applied a range of supervised and unsupervised machine learning models to real world data and test predictions. Prior to GA, I learned fundamentals of data visualization and practiced extensively in Tableau.
